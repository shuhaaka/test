Project Management Specialization for Master Students

Why Project Management?

In today’s business world, the demand for skilled project managers is urgent and growing. About 80 percent of all major projects fail because of cost overruns or substandard quality. Less than 20 percent of all major projects are completed on time. Key projects are expanding not only in terms of numbers, but also in terms of scale, scope, and medium. Projects are stretching the capabilities of for-profit and non-profit organization managers, by rapidly moving from the physical to the virtual, accelerating the pace of globalization, and changing the way in which we do business.

Summary of Credits and Calendar

During Modules B, C, and D, Master students must take an additional 6-credit (3-course) sequence of project management courses to qualify for the Project Management Specialization. The project management sequence of courses is custom designed to rapidly build up your skills in project management. These 6 credits (3 courses) are taken on top of the 39 credits required for the Master programs. The Project Management Specialization is offered at the Boston, San Francisco and London campuses (subject to demand; min. 40 students). Therefore students undertaking the specialization must be present at one of the participating campuses during Modules B, C, and D. Upon successful completion of these 6 credits (with a minimum of a B-grade in each course), students will receive a Specialization in Project Management in addition to their Master degree. (Note: students must successfully complete both the Master program and the additional requirements to be awarded the specialization).

  • Epicenter of the tech world

    San Francisco is the embodiment of the digitally driven age in which we currently live. The city has long been powered by a startup economy, earning itself a global reputation for creativity and technological wizardry. Having seen a generation of determined entrepreneurs build huge business empires out of garages and college dorm rooms, San Francisco has become the world’s premier destination for those wanting to push the boundaries of the possible. Silicon Valley, just south of the city, is the world’s leading tech hub, home to anyone who is anyone in the digital world, including Facebook, eBay, Intel, and LinkedIn.

    Throw out the old corporate handbooks

    A diverse population, startup-driven economy, and a famously informal approach to corporate etiquette mean that many conventional business rules have no place in San Francisco. Although huge corporations tend to get all the headlines, 85% of the companies in the Bay Area have less than ten employees. This blend of small and mighty makes for a unique business environment where forward thinking and creativity are the most highly prized credentials.
